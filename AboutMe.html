My name is Harold Solomon and I have been involved in engineering since high school, having won second place in state at the Illinois Drafting Educators Association Competition and being a coach for my high school's Engineering Drafters club. I have been CAD modeling ever since and it is still something I enjoy doing when I begin new projects. With my hometown in the Chicago suburn of South Elgin, I have lived my entire life in Illinois.

I graduated from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in May 2019 with a Bachelor of Science in Engineering Mechanics and a secondary field in Autonomous Controls and Robotics. During my time at Illinois, I was able to grow into the free-thinker that I am today. I am very enthusiastic about mechanical engineering and technology as well as topics in the LGBT+ community, which I have come to love for the people I met within and for the pride I find in this identity.

An Innovator in Engineering and Technology

When I first started attending U of I as a student in Mechanical Science and Engineering (MechSE for short), I knew that I wanted to use the knowledge that I gained to expand the reach of engineering and tech. With my background in mechanics, I received a thorough understanding of the physical world. But to promote my endeavors even further, I decided to choose a focus for my major which was relevant to machine learning, which has experienced an upsurgence in popularity in many industries in recent years. From these aspects of my education, in addition to my own efforts through projects, I have incorporated the two fields into my skill set.

During my undergrad, I worked on seven different robots ranging from walkers to collaborators to drones; I worked in a robotics laboratory, researching the dynamics and control of search and rescue robots by performing tests and simulations; I delved into artificial intelligence to increase my knowledge and experience with reinforcement learning and neural networks. I have been involved with trailblazers and pioneers and I wish to extend what I have learned to those around me.

An Effective Leader and an Advocate for Diversity

I have had the honor of participating on executive boards for organizations on campus. As the President of Out in STEM (oSTEM), I was able to promote LGBT+ identites in STEM fields. While overseeing the organization, I have seen the community come to be embraced at U of I and I watched members attend career fairs and get jobs and internships without having to feel insecure about their identities. I am happy knowing that I was able to contribute to the acceptance of sexual and gender diversity on campus and to all the companies and universities where our members ended up.

I was also the Vice President of Membership of Pi Tau Sigma Mechanical Engineering Honor Society where I ensured that the organization retained a high volume of members. When first beginning my role, I found a way to perform my duties in a way that would draw in members while remaining significantly below the budget for the semester. I was able to bring in workshops for members to gain useful knowledge and skills so that they could leave meetings with a better grasp on how to navigate their careers. This role was exciting for me because it gave me the opportunity to reach out around campus to bring in guests for our society.
